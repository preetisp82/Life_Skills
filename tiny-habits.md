# Tiny Habits

## 1. In this video, what was the most interesting story or idea for you?

The most interesting idea was that small habits can lead to big changes over time. Starting with tiny actions makes it easier to build lasting habits.

## 2. How can you use B = MAP to make new habits easier? What are M, A and P?

B = MAP means a behavior happens when all three are present:

* **M** - Motivation
* **A** - Ability
* **P** - Prompt

## 3. Why is it important to "Shine" or celebrate after each successful completion of a habit?

Celebrating creates a positive feeling, which helps the brain remember the habit and makes it easier to repeat consistently.

## 4. In this video, what was the most interesting story or idea for you?

The most interesting idea was that improving by just 1% every day leads to significant progress over time through consistent effort.

## 5. What is the book's perspective about Identity?

The book explains that lasting habits come from changing your identity. Instead of focusing only on goals, become the kind of person who naturally follows those habits.

## 6. What is the book's perspective on how to make a habit easier to do?

* Start with a small habit.
* Reduce the effort required.
* Prepare everything in advance.
* Practice consistently.

## 7. What is the book's perspective on how to make a habit harder to do?

* Remove the cue.
* Increase the effort required.
* Make the habit inconvenient.
* Reduce the rewards.

## 8. Pick one habit that you would like to do more of. What steps can you take?

**Habit:** Daily coding practice

* Keep my laptop ready before study time.
* Start with just 20 minutes every day.
* Practice at the same time daily.
* Track my progress on a calendar.
* Celebrate after completing each session.

## 9. Pick one habit that you would like to eliminate or do less of. What steps can you take?

**Habit:** Spending too much time on social media

* Remove social media apps from the home screen.
* Turn off notifications.
* Keep the phone away while studying.
* Set a daily time limit.
* Reward myself for staying within the limit.
